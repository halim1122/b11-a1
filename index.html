Pillars of "Bangladesh 2.0"

Innovation

The pillar of Innovation in the vision of "Bangladesh 2.0" is about fostering a culture of creativity, research, and technological advancement to drive sustainable growth and global competitiveness.

Sustainability

The pillar of Sustainability in the vision of "Bangladesh 2.0" focuses on creating a resilient, eco-friendly, and resource-efficient nation.creating a resilient, eco-friendly, and resource-efficient nation.

Infrastructure

The Infrastructure Pillar of "Bangladesh 2.0" envisions a robust, modern, and sustainable framework to support economic growth, enhance connectivity, and improve the quality of life for citizens.

Empowerment

The Empowerment Pillar of "Bangladesh 2.0" emphasizes enabling individuals and communities to achieve their full potential through access to education, skills, resources, and opportunities.

The Vision of Future

Bangladesh 2.0 aims to redefine the nation’s trajectory by embracing cutting-edge technology, promoting inclusive growth, and ensuring sustainability. With initiatives in renewable energy, digital transformation, and robust infrastructure, the country is set to become a global model of development.

Dr. Muhammad Yunus is a Bangladeshi economist, social entrepreneur, and Nobel Peace Prize laureate (2006) renowned for pioneering microfinance and founding Grameen Bank. His work empowers the poor, particularly women, by providing small, collateral-free loans, helping millions escape poverty. He also introduced the concept of social business, addressing societal issues through sustainable enterprises. Globally influential, he authored notable books like "Banker to the Poor" and continues to inspire economic inclusion and sustainable development.
